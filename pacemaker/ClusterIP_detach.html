Actually in this case, a1 was the DC. So the calculations by pengine were carried out on a1 before stopping ClusterIP on a2. After stopping ClusterIP on a2, ClusterIP on a1 was started. As a conclusion, pacemaker tried to remove(stop) ClusterIP on the failure node. So even if stonith is disabled, ClusterIP on the failure node is removed (at least tried to be removed), then assigned to the new active node.
